WE Bubbles · Community Learning · 0→1

A home for a community too big for WhatsApp

A community of 8,000 women, looking for a place to actually learn together.

RoleProduct UX Lead TeamMe + 1 frontend developer Timeline3 months · MVP live at we-learn-v002.vercel.app
01 · The problem

A place to talk isn't a place to learn

WhatsApp gave them a place to talk, not to learn. Content got lost. Groups multiplied with no center. Anyone could "join" with zero real commitment.

02 · Four pain points, four screens
Scattered groups → Landing Page: all Bubbles, one place
Unmanageable content → Per-section syllabus editing
Fake commitment → AI Commitment Check on Join
Lost resources → Dedicated Resources tab
03 · The sharpest call
Joining isn't supposed to be easy here

The Commitment Check asks how many hours a woman can really give, and pushes back if it's not enough. A Bubble only works if its members show up. So the filter had to sit before membership, not after it fails.

04 · Impact

A live MVP, built by a team of two

  • → Live MVP, shipped in 3 months, one developer.
  • → Early rollout. Usage data still ahead.

Structure didn't replace the community. It gave it room to grow.

Building inside the community you're designing for is a shortcut to the truth, and here, to the right kind of friction.
