01: The Mission

Define. Visualize. Don't add complexity.

Define and visualize an end-to-end developer experience for CAP application and agent development across VS Code, Cursor, and Windsurf, complementing the SAP Build vision. Complement SAP Build. Don't add complexity.

Read

SAP Build had grown into a family of tools: Build Apps, Build Process Automation, Build Work Zone. Each served a purpose. But together, they'd become hard to navigate, even for people inside SAP. Our job was to find a way through.

02: User Research · Developer Interviews

We went to talk to developers. They were exhausted.

We interviewed internal developers, engineering managers, and product managers. What we heard wasn't a feature request. It was frustration.

Read

The confusion wasn't just about naming. It was about cognitive load: developers spending mental energy navigating SAP's tool landscape instead of building. Every context switch had a cost.

"I gave up a long time ago trying to figure out what everything was with the word Build in it."

DJ Adams · Developer Advocate, SAP

03: Mid-Research · Strategic Pivot

Halfway through, the brief changed.

Mid-research, SAP expanded the scope: the experience needed to serve not just developers, but business users too. That's when the real design challenge came into focus.

Developers switch tools. Business users abandon them.

Read

Developers are comfortable moving between IDEs, terminals, and platforms. It's part of how they work. Business users like Sarah aren't. Every additional tool is friction. Every context switch is a reason to stop.

The solution couldn't just be better tooling for developers. It had to be invisible infrastructure for everyone else.

04: Design Principle · White-Label Approach

The answer wasn't another tool. It was one seamless experience.

Instead of building a new surface, we embedded the experience inside what users already trusted. A white-label approach: powerful technology, invisible seams, one familiar environment.

Read

White labeling meant that whether a developer was in VS Code or a business user was in SAP Build, they were experiencing the same underlying capability, without knowing it. The tool faded away. The work stayed.

05: Two Concepts · Design Decision

Two concepts. One kept users where they belonged.

We explored two directions. Both worked technically. Only one felt right for the user.

AVercel-first ✕ Not chosen
v0.dev

Powerful and familiar to developers, but it asked business users to leave their environment to finish the job.

BSAP-first ✓ Chosen
build.sap.com

Same technology underneath, but the experience never left SAP. The moment a business user has to leave, we've already lost them.

Read

Concept A started in Vercel: powerful, familiar to developers, but required business users to leave their environment. Concept B kept everything inside SAP. The technology was the same. The experience was completely different.

We chose B because the moment a business user has to leave SAP, we've already lost them.

06: Business Impact · Deploy to BTP

One button, in the right place, made the business goal reachable.

Through synthesis of our interviews, I proposed Deploy to BTP: a single action that takes a business user from "I built something" to "it's live," without ever needing to understand the infrastructure behind it. It became the project's most critical business decision.

Read

SAP's business model depends on deployments to BTP. Every app that lives outside the ecosystem is revenue that doesn't happen. By keeping the experience inside SAP and making deployment a single, trusted action, we turned a UX decision into a business outcome.

07: Impact · Reflection

The work found its way forward.

The solution was presented at an internal workshop in Germany, and received with enthusiasm. Working closely with a PM and engineering lead, we proposed a direction: embedded, seamless, deployment-first, in collaboration with Vercel. Shortly after, SAP launched a platform moving in exactly this direction at Sapphire.

  • →Deploy to BTP moved from a research proposal to one of the project's defining decisions.
  • →The pivot from standalone to white-label, embedded became a principle that shaped the platform well beyond our scope.
